Special characters

These characters are used in all kinds of different ways depending on the programming language. You can't live without them. You can start the lesson by using go right and then enter or snap. You can stop the time by saying Escape enter. If you wish to leave this lesson, you can press the 'Close' button, or simply say Control Whale to press Ctrl+W to close this tab.

00:00.0

Punctuation is important both for programming and for writing. . is dot, , is comma, ? is question mark and ! is bang. As you can see, these are fairly logical, just like : being colon and ; being semicolon.
Quoting things can be handy as well, and you can use ` back tick, ' quote and " dubquote to place these characters. You might have to say space after these sounds to make them show up
Next up are the braces. There are multiple kinds of braces: {} which are left brace and right brace. () which are left paren and right paren, and [] which are left square and right square and <>, which technically aren't braces, but can be activated using left angle and right angle respectively.
Now there are a number of miscellaneous signs that I couldn't really fit in a category, so I'll just list them out here. = equals, _ down score, - minus, + plus, * star, # hash, / slash, \ back slash, $ dollar, % percent, ^ caret, & and sign, | pipe, @ at sign and ~ for tilde.

This is based on the knausj_talon repository. As with almost anything else in Talon, you can change the words for specific symbols around if they do not recognize well.
They can be changed here: core/keys/keys.py.
